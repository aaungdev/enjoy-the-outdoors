Moraine Lake (Canada)

Moraine Lake, situated in Banff National Park in Alberta, Canada, is renowned for its striking turquoise waters and breathtaking mountain scenery. Located in the Valley of the Ten Peaks at an elevation of 1,885 meters (6,183 feet), this glacially-fed lake owes its vibrant color to light refracting off fine rock particles. The lake's hue peaks in late June, attracting tourists, hikers, and photographers. Moraine Lake offers a range of outdoor activities, including canoeing and hiking, making it a quintessential symbol of the natural beauty found in the Canadian Rockies.

Central Park (New York)

Central Park, in Manhattan, New York City, is an iconic urban park designed by Frederick Law Olmsted and Calvert Vaux. The park's construction began in 1858, transforming a swampy area into a beautifully landscaped space that opened in 1859. Covering 843 acres, it features meadows, woodlands, lakes, and various architectural elements. Central Park has become a cultural and recreational hub, hosting events and offering a serene escape for millions of visitors each year, symbolizing New York City's commitment to green spaces.
